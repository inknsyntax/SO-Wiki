What is This?

Sensory Overload

Sensory overload occurs when one or more of the body's five senses (sight, sound, smell, touch, taste) receive more information than the brain can process, leading to extreme feelings of discomfort, overwhelm, or stress.

The Experience

Designed to feel intentionally unstable without being unplayable. Visual glitches, chromatic shifts, and disorienting effects create tension. But underneath the chaos is solid, responsive gameplay.

Disorientation Mechanics

Core Gameplay

Players navigate through environments while visual and audio cues intentionally mislead their perception. The goal is to maintain focus despite constant sensory interference.

Perception Shifts

The game subtly alters colors, timing, and spatial awareness. What seems stable is actually slowly rotating. What appears far might be closer than it seems.

Recovery Mechanics

Moments of stability are as important as chaos. Strategic safe zones allow players to recalibrate their perception before the next wave of disorientation.
